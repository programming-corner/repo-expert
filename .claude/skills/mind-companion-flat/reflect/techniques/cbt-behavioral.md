# 🔄 CBT + Behavioral Analysis

---

## ━━━ MODULE 1 — COGNITIVE REFRAMING ━━━

> Goal: help the person see their thought from a different angle —
> not by dismissing it, but by examining what's actually true.

### The 10 Cognitive Distortions (internal map — never recited as a list)

Use these to recognize what's happening, then gently name it:

| Distortion | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| All-or-nothing | "I always fail" / "I never get it right" |
| Catastrophizing | "This will ruin everything" |
| Mind reading | "They think I'm stupid" |
| Emotional reasoning | "I feel like a failure so I must be one" |
| Should statements | "I should be over this by now" |
| Labeling | "I'm a loser" / "I'm broken" |
| Personalization | "It's all my fault" |
| Filtering | Only seeing the negative, ignoring the positive |
| Jumping to conclusions | Assuming the worst without evidence |
| Discounting positives | "That doesn't count" |

When you notice one → don't name it directly yet. Explore first.

---

### Workflow

**Step 1 — Catch the thought**
> "What's the thought that keeps coming back — the one that feels the loudest?"
> "Can you say it out loud, exactly as it sounds in your head?"

Let them say it fully. Don't rush to fix it. Reflect it back exactly.
> "So the thought is: '[their words].' Is that right?"

**Step 2 — Examine the evidence (Evidence Court)**
> "If that thought were on trial — what's the evidence that supports it?"
> "And what's the evidence against it — even small things?"

Never dismiss the supporting evidence. Hold both sides genuinely.

**Step 3 — The journalist test**
> "Imagine a journalist investigating this thought.
> What would they actually find if they looked at the facts?"

This creates distance from the thought without invalidating the feeling.

**Step 4 — "And then what?" chain**
Used for catastrophizing:
> "If that happened — then what?"
> "And if that happened — then what?"
Keep going until they reach the actual fear underneath.
Usually 3–4 steps reveals the real root.

**Step 5 — Build the reframe**
Never hand them the reframe. Guide them to find it:
> "Given all of that — is there another way to look at this situation?
> Not the positive spin — just a more complete picture."

If they struggle:
> "What would you say to a close friend who told you this exact thought?"

**Step 6 — The thought record (5 columns)**
For deeper sessions — guide through each column one at a time:

```
① Situation    → What happened? Where, when, who?
② Emotion      → What did you feel? How intense (0–10)?
③ Hot thought  → What was the loudest thought in that moment?
④ Evidence     → For it / Against it
⑤ Reframe      → A more balanced way to see it (in their words)
```

Never show all 5 columns at once — walk through them conversationally.

## ━━━ MODULE 2 — BEHAVIORAL ANALYSIS ━━━

> Goal: map the pattern — what triggers the behavior,
> what happens during it, what follows — and find the hidden function.

### The ABC Model

```
A → Antecedent   What happened right before?
B → Behavior     What did you do / not do?
C → Consequence  What followed — short term and long term?
```

**Step 1 — Describe one specific instance**
> "Think of the last time this happened — one specific moment, not the pattern in general.
> Walk me through it from the beginning."

Specific is everything. Patterns are generalizations — instances are real.

**Step 2 — Map the ABC**
> "What was happening right before — what triggered it?"
> "What exactly did you do in that moment?"
> "What happened right after — how did it feel short term?"
> "And what's the longer-term consequence you've noticed?"

Reflect the full map back before moving on.

**Step 3 — Find the function**
Every behavior serves a purpose — even the ones we hate.
> "What does this behavior give you — even if the cost is high?
> Does it give you relief? Control? Distance? Comfort?"

Never judge the function. Understand it first.

**Step 4 — The habit loop**
> "So the pattern seems to be:
> [trigger] → [behavior] → [short-term relief / reward].
> Does that feel accurate?"

If yes → explore how to interrupt the loop at the trigger stage.
If no → go back and listen more carefully.

**Step 5 — Cost-benefit reflection**
> "If you looked at this pattern honestly —
> what has it cost you? And what has it given you?
> Not what you think you should say — what's actually true?"

Hold both sides without judgment.

**Step 6 — Pattern timeline (for recurring patterns)**
> "How long has this pattern been with you?
> When do you first remember it showing up?"

This often reveals the origin — which is not about blame, but about understanding.

## ━━━ REFLECTION RULES FOR THIS MODULE ━━━

- Always explore before naming any distortion or pattern
- When naming → always as observation, never as fact:
  "I notice something that sounds like [X] — does that resonate?"
- Never say "you're catastrophizing" — say "I notice the thought jumps quite far ahead — does it?"
- After reframe is found → don't celebrate it immediately
  Ask first: "How does that feel — does it land as true, or does part of you resist it?"
- Resistance is information — explore it, don't push through it
